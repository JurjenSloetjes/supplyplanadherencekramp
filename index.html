Understanding Our Performance Landscape

Supply Plan Adherence (SPA) is a foundational metric that quantifies how closely our operational execution aligns with strategic planning. It meticulously compares what we planned to do — in both timing and quantity, originating from the Slim4 order advice — with what was actually achieved by the time items are restocked on the warehouse shelf. This infographic delves into the critical aspects of SPA, its measurement through On-Time In-Full (OTIF), and its profound impact on our operational efficiency and customer satisfaction.

Why SPA Matters

High SPA signals a predictable, efficient, and robust supply process. It mitigates risks, optimizes resource allocation, enhances cross-functional collaboration, and is vital for internal operational discipline. Ultimately, it ensures we meet our commitments reliably.

Drilling Down: OTIF Score Impact

The final OTIF score is a product of the On-Time Supplied Ratio and the In-Full Supplied Ratio. Even a partial miss in one dimension heavily penalizes the overall score.

Perfect Scenario

100% On-Time & 100% In-Full results in 100% OTIF.

In-Full, Not On-Time

If 100% quantity arrives, but late, the On-Time Supplied Ratio is 0%, leading to 0% OTIF.

Partially On-Time, Not In-Full (64% OTIF)

Delivering 80% of quantity on time yields 64% OTIF.

Partially On-Time, In-Full (80% OTIF)

Delivering 100% of quantity, but only 80% on time yields 80% OTIF.

Deconstructing OTIF

OTIF's strength lies in its two core components:

  • On-Time Supplied Quantity: Quantity that arrived on or before the Expected On Shelf Date. If late, this component is zero for that order line, irrespective of quantity.
  • In-Full Supplied Quantity: The lesser of quantity ordered (Total Advised Quantity) and quantity delivered (Delivered Quantity). Over-deliveries do not improve this score beyond the planned amount.

The final OTIF Supply Score is calculated by multiplying the On-Time Supplied Ratio by the In-Full Supplied Ratio. This multiplicative approach means excellence is required on both fronts. A 90% on-time and 90% in-full results in only 81% OTIF, not 90%.

Key Calculation Principle:

OTIF Score = (On-Time Supplied Qty / Total Advised Qty) * (In-Full Supplied Qty / Total Advised Qty)

The SPA Process & The Role of Slim4

The journey to Supply Plan Adherence is a structured process, heavily reliant on the initial planning data from our Slim4 system. This "black box" approach measures the ultimate outcome: items being ready and available in stock. It encompasses supplier performance, transport, and internal warehouse operations. The accuracy of Lead Time data in Slim4 is crucial for a meaningful Expected On Shelf Date and thus a fair SPA assessment.

1. Slim4 System
Generates Order Advice (Total Advised Quantity)
2. Planning Phase
Define Expected On Shelf Date (Planned Order Date + Total Lead Time)
3. Execution Tracking
Monitor Delivered Quantity & Actual On Shelf Date (OMS status)
4. SPA Calculation
Determine OTIF score (On-Time & In-Full)

What's Not Included in this Calculation?

To keep the SPA metric focused and accurate, certain types of orders and items are intentionally excluded from the calculation. This ensures we are measuring our core replenishment performance against external suppliers.

Specific Warehouses

Warehouses '100', '250', and '400' are excluded because the 'actual on shelf date' is not measured for these locations, making an accurate SPA calculation impossible.

Internal Stock Movements

Orders between our own internal entities or inter-company transfers are not included, as SPA focuses on external supplier performance.

Kit Products

Products designated as 'KITs' are excluded because they are not ordered, but their individual parts are.

New or Unplanned Products

Articles with no ordering schedule are not captured in the overdue part. The SPA measurement begins for these items as soon as their first order is placed, as that is where there plan starts.

What SPA Tells Us About Our Business

SPA is a powerful diagnostic tool. It doesn't solve problems on its own, but it gives us precise insights into where our supply chain is succeeding and where we need to focus our improvement efforts.

⚙️

Are our internal processes efficient?

By comparing the planned on-shelf date with the actual, SPA highlights delays in our transport or warehouse inbound processes.

🎯

Is our planning data accurate?

Consistently low SPA can indicate that the lead times and order parameters in Slim4 need to be reviewed and updated.

📊

How predictable is our supply chain?

A high SPA score gives us confidence in our stock availability forecasts and our ability to meet commitments without costly surprises.
