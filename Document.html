Understanding Our Performance Landscape

Supply Plan Adherence (SPA) is a foundational metric that quantifies how closely our operational execution aligns with strategic planning. It meticulously compares what we planned to do — in both timing and quantity, originating from the Slim4 order advice — with what was actually achieved by the time items are restocked on the warehouse shelf. This infographic delves into the critical aspects of SPA, its measurement through On-Time In-Full (OTIF), and its profound impact on our operational efficiency and customer satisfaction.

Why SPA Matters

High SPA signals a predictable, efficient, and robust supply process. It mitigates risks, optimizes resource allocation, enhances cross-functional collaboration, and is vital for internal operational discipline. Ultimately, it ensures we meet our commitments reliably.

Drilling Down: OTIF Score Impact

The final OTIF score is a product of the On-Time Supplied Ratio and the In-Full Supplied Ratio. Even a partial miss in one dimension heavily penalizes the overall score.

Perfect Scenario

100% On-Time & 100% In-Full results in 100% OTIF.

In-Full, Not On-Time

Even if 100% quantity arrives, if it's late, the On-Time Supplied Ratio is 0%, leading to 0% OTIF.

Partially On-Time, Not In-Full (64% OTIF)

Delivering 80% of quantity on time (80% In-Full Supplied Ratio, 80% On-Time Supplied Ratio) yields 64% OTIF.

Partially On-Time, In-Full (80% OTIF)

Delivering 100% of quantity, but only 80% on time (80% On-Time Supplied Ratio, 100% In-Full Supplied Ratio) yields 80% OTIF.

Deconstructing OTIF: The Strict Standard

OTIF's strength lies in its two core components:

  • On-Time Supplied Quantity: Quantity that arrived on or before the Expected On Shelf Date. If late, this component is zero for that order line, irrespective of quantity.
  • In-Full Supplied Quantity: The lesser of quantity ordered (Total Advised Quantity) and quantity delivered (Delivered Quantity). Over-deliveries do not improve this score beyond the planned amount.

The final OTIF Supply Score is calculated by multiplying the On-Time Supplied Ratio by the In-Full Supplied Ratio. This multiplicative approach means excellence is required on both fronts. A 90% on-time and 90% in-full results in only 81% OTIF, not 90%.

Key Calculation Principle:

OTIF Supply Score = (On-Time Supplied Quantity / Total Advised Quantity) * (In-Full Supplied Quantity / Total Advised Quantity)

This ensures shortfalls in either timing or quantity significantly impact the overall score, driving comprehensive performance improvements.

Strategic Perspective on SPA

Strengths

  • Clear KPI definition (SPA via OTIF).
  • System-driven planning (Slim4).
  • Holistic view covering entire supply process.
  • Ability to pinpoint execution gaps.

Weaknesses (Areas for Vigilance)

  • Sensitivity to late deliveries (0% OTIF impact).
  • Dependency on accurate master data in Slim4 (Lead Times).
  • Not punishing too much quantities.

Opportunities

  • Continuously refine Slim4 master data for better planning.
  • Enhance supplier collaboration and performance monitoring.
  • Optimize warehouse inbound processes.
  • Leverage SPA insights for better forecasting.

Threats (Risks of Low SPA)

  • Increased operational costs (expediting, excess buffer).
  • Reduced customer satisfaction due to stockouts/delays.
  • Inefficient resource utilization.
  • Erosion of supply chain predictability and reliability.
